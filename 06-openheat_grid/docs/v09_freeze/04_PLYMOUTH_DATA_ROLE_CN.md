# Plymouth data role in the heat-stress prediction system

Your Plymouth dissertation is already a completed mobile monitoring + GEMA study. It should not be repeated as the main OpenHeat analysis.

## What Plymouth proves

- You can design a route/POI-based mobile monitoring protocol.
- You can collect pedestrian-scale Tair, RH, globe temperature, wind, GPS, GVI and perception data.
- You know how to derive thermal indices such as PET and Tmrt from field measurements.
- You can handle POI-level exposure windows and QA/QC.

## What Plymouth does not prove

- It does not validate Singapore tropical heatwave prediction.
- It does not validate high-WBGT thresholds.
- It does not provide Toa Payoh local calibration.

## Best wording

> Plymouth provides methodological evidence for pedestrian-scale exposure measurement and sensor-to-index processing. The Toa Payoh forecast component should be calibrated against Singapore official weather and WBGT observations.

## How to use it in portfolio

Put it as a short Methodological Foundation section, then move the main contribution to the forecast system.
